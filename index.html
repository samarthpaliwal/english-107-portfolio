About Me

I am a first-year Computer Science student at the University of Arizona. I like working with technology, learning new skills, and going for walks when I need a break. Before this course, I saw writing as something I did only for school. I focused on grammar and small errors instead of thinking about purpose, audience, or structure.

Introduction to My Portfolio

My Learning Journey

At the start of English 107, I wrote most assignments in one sitting. I checked for spelling and grammar and then turned them in. I did not think much about how my choices on structure, tone, or evidence affected the reader. Over the semester, this changed. I began to plan my writing in advance and to see revision as a normal part of the process.

Peer review and class workshops helped me the most. When other students read my work, I could see where my ideas were clear and where they caused confusion. I also saw how different genres, like a literacy narrative and an infographic, ask for different choices. This pushed me to adjust my style and layout based on the task and the audience.

This portfolio brings together the projects that show this growth. Each artifact connects to at least one course goal and shows how my writing changed through feedback, revision, and reflection.

Conclusion

These five artifacts show how my writing changed during this semester. At the beginning, I did not plan much and I revised only for small mistakes. Now I think more about audience, genre, and purpose, and I see writing as a process with several steps.

The literacy narrative and the infographic pushed me in different ways, but the same habits helped in both projects. Careful reading, clear structure, and steady revision made my work stronger. I still want to improve my sentence flow, my analysis, and my use of sources in other classes.

My plan is to start major assignments earlier, ask for feedback, and give myself time for at least one full round of revision. This course showed me that writing is a skill I can keep building. These habits will help me in future English courses, in Computer Science, and anywhere clear communication matters.

‚ÄúWriting, like climbing, happens one step at a time. Each draft brings me a little closer to the summit.‚Äù
